Richard "Dick" Bong

The greatest fighter pilot in American history

His life through the years:

  • ✈️ September, 1920 - Born in Superior, Wisconsin
  • ✈️ May, 1941 - Leaves his family in Poplar, Wisconsin to enlist in the Army Aviation Cadet Program
  • ✈️ January, 1942 - Promoted to the rank of Second Liutenant and awarded his pilot wings
  • ✈️ December, 1942 - Records first aerial victory of World War Two at the battle of Buna-Gona, earning him a Silver Star
  • ✈️ July, 1943 - Shoots down 4 Japanese fighters, earning him a cross for Distinguished Service
  • ✈️ August, 1943 - Promoted to Captain
  • ✈️ November, 1943 - Meets Marjorie Vattendahl while on leave and the two begin to date
  • ✈️ April, 1944 - Shoots down his 27th enemy fighter, surpassing the American aerial victory record. He is then promoted to Major.
  • ✈️ December, 1944 - Receives Medal of Honor from General Douglas MacArthur in special ceremony
  • ✈️ February, 1945 - Returns home from the war and marries Marjorie Vattendahl
  • ✈️ August, 1945 - Becomes a test pilot for the Lockheed Corporation and experiences a fatal crash at age 24
